Why you can’t find your perfect Data Scientist

 

Posted by Kylie Reiri on 14 May 2017

I am a trained statistician. I’ve had a few titles throughout my career; Statistical Analyst, Researcher, Evaluator, Data Scientist. What did I do differently in these roles? Nothing.

I don’t love the title Data Scientist, but I have to admit, of all of these title, it is the one that immediately informs people of what I do: learn from data to make more informed decisions.

The downside for recruiters with the name is it’s really just a catch all for a wide range of capabilities that are required to learn from data. The capabilities are so varied it seems strange to think recruiters look for that perfect data scientist at all.

To get the point across quickly, here is my oversimplification of the capabilities needed to learn from data to make more informed decisions.

Data architecture helps us collect, store, organise and integrate data.

Software engineering helps us improve and develop new tools to make data science more efficient.

Computer science helps us write slick code.

Mathematics and statistics helps us understand the patterns in data.

Data translation and interpretation helps us understand what the data is telling us.

Data story-telling helps us relate to others what we see in the data, through words.

Data visualisation helps us show people what the data is telling us, through pictures.

Domain knowledge helps us understand the underlying data and what questions we should ask.

Business influence helps us act on insights.

Have you ever met someone who has all of these capabilities? I haven’t. And these don’t capture the soft-skills you’re probably after too.

But there is good news. One person doesn’t need to master all of these capabilities. It is enough for data scientists to master just one or two really well, and know just enough about the others to know they are all essential to a successful data science project.

So what does this mean for you? Stop looking for one person who can do it all. Understand all of the capabilities you need for your project and build a team around them.

Be specific about what you’re looking for. If you have plenty of statistical skills, hire a someone with software engineering skills to help make the teams code more efficient. If you don’t have great story telling skills and you’re wondering why your insights aren’t being used, hire a great story teller.

Building a great data science team requires a range of capabilities, don't try and find them all in one person.
